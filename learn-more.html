Tech Specs

The Odyssey used no microprocessor — it ran entirely on analog circuits. Players moved dots on screen using controllers with horizontal and vertical knobs.

It shipped with dice, play money, and transparent screen overlays — blurring the line between board games and video games.

Iconic Games

Table Tennis

Inspired Atari's Pong and launched the entire arcade and home gaming industries.

Haunted House

One of the earliest uses of a screen overlay to create atmosphere — a primitive but creative approach to game design.

Legacy

The Odyssey proved that interactive electronic entertainment could work at home. Without it, there would be no Atari, no Nintendo, no PlayStation.

Ralph Baer is widely recognized as the 'Father of Video Games,' and the Odyssey's influence echoes through every console that followed.
